Rexburg Adventures

My idea was to create a website called Rexburg Adventures, designed specifically for college students looking for fun things to do in and around Rexburg. The goal is to make it easier for students to find affordable, exciting activities without having to spend hours searching or relying on word of mouth. The site includes categories like outdoor adventures, local events, date ideas, free activities, restaurant guides, and student discounts. It will also feature user reviews, maps, photos, and a weekly “Top Picks” list for quick inspiration. What makes Rexburg Adventures unique is that it’s made by and for college students—it understands the local lifestyle, budget, and need for spontaneous fun. It’s not just a list of places; it’s a community-driven hub that helps students make the most of their college experience in Rexburg.

Pick of the Week

On campus at BYU-Idaho there are many fun things to do! Check out their Website to see what fun things they have to offer this week!

More Activities

The Rexburg Nature Park, located less than 2 miles from BYU-I campus, has an amazing 10-hole frisbee golf course open to the public! Just bring your own discs or rent them from the Outdoor Rec Center and play for free!

Gravity Factory trampoline park is one of Rexburg’s greatest hubs due to its usability for all ages! Attractions include a large trampoline arena, a ninja warrior course, a foam pit, and dodgeball!

The St. Anthony Ice Caves, located about 35 miles outside of Rexburg, are an awesome way to spend a day out in nature. It’s a short hike, followed by some crawling and sliding around inside on the huge sheets of ice!

Roots and Boots offers Country Dancing Wednesday-Saturday from 8pm till Midnight. Price of entry is about $6 but varies with form of payment. Get your boots and cowboy hats on for a night of fun dancing either inside or under the stars!

The Rock Gym is an indoor rock-climbing facility that is equipped with harnesses, weights, bouldering, and training areas. Grab a group of friends and climb away! Fear of heights: Not recommended.
